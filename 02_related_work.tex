\section{Related Work}

Operating room scheduling has been widely studied \cite{AlAmin2024}. Among heuristic approaches, Kamran et al. \cite{Kamran2020} applied a column-generation-heuristic-based Benders' decomposition on adaptive allocation scheduling of patients in operating rooms, while Wang et al. \cite{Wang2024} developed a Wasserstein distributionally robust optimization model with a rolling horizon rescheduling scheme for surgery scheduling involving both elective and emergency patients.

AI-based methods have also been used. Lex et al. \cite{Lex2025} combined machine learning prediction with scheduling optimization for elective orthopedic surgery. Zhao et al. \cite{Zhao2025} integrated reinforcement learning with column generation for OR scheduling. El-Balka et al. \cite{ElBalka2025} proposed a dynamic scheduling strategy combining explainable AI with a fuzzy engine for intraday scheduling. Eshghali et al. \cite{Eshghali2024} used random forest with genetic algorithm and particle swarm optimization for elective and emergency patient scheduling.

Stochastic approaches address uncertainties in surgical scheduling \cite{Bernardelli2024}. M'Hallah and Visintin \cite{MHallah2019} applied stochastic integer programming for the Master Surgical Schedule. Zhang et al. \cite{Zhang2021} combined Markov decision processes with stochastic programming for surgery scheduling.

Among exact methods, Çelik et al. \cite{Celik2023} used a two-stage stochastic MIP with progressive hedging. Schneider et al. \cite{Schneider2020} employed a MILP for surgery group scheduling with downstream resources. Miao and Wang \cite{Miao2021} proposed a MIP for elective and emergency scheduling with waiting time limits. Akbarzadeh and Maenhout \cite{Akbarzadeh2025} developed a branch-price-and-cut algorithm for patient and surgeon scheduling. Makboul et al. \cite{Makboul2025} applied multiobjective $\epsilon$-constraint robust optimization for the master surgical schedule.
Xue et al. \cite{Nash2025} addressed multi-resource constrained elective surgical scheduling using Nash equilibrium, while \cite{IntegratedRobust2025} applied distributionally robust optimization to integrated inpatient and ambulatory surgery scheduling.

Closest to our work, Spratt and Kozan \cite{Spratt2019} proposed constructive heuristics with real-time reactive strategies for surgical case sequencing across multiple operating rooms.


Yang et al. \cite{Yang2025} recently integrated patient satisfaction into OR scheduling using a multinomial logit model to capture surgeon and date preferences. Our work differs by integrating patient choice directly into the scheduling process: the model proposes two to three feasible slots per patient and guarantees at least one option for every patient.
